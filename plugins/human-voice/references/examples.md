# Human Voice — Reference Examples

Comprehensive before/after examples showing what sounds human vs. AI across every communication context. Use these as the benchmark.

---

## Why AI Writing Sounds Like AI

AI writing has structural tells that appear consistently:
- Announces what it will say, says it, then summarizes ("In this article, we will explore…")
- Opens every paragraph with a connector: "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally"
- Hedges every claim: "could potentially be considered," "it may be worth noting"
- Uses passive voice by default: "it has been determined" instead of "we decided"
- Uniform sentence rhythm throughout — every sentence the same length
- Inflates simple ideas with formal vocabulary nobody uses in conversation

As Paul Graham puts it: *"Written language is more complex, which makes it more work to read. The complex sentences and fancy words give you, the writer, the false impression that you're saying more than you actually are."*

---

## The Master Rule

Read it out loud. If you stumble, the sentence is wrong. If you wouldn't say it to a friend, rewrite it as if you would.

---

## Banned Phrases (Complete List)

These are statistically overrepresented in AI output. Cut every one:

| Category | Cut These |
|---|---|
| Filler openers | "It's important to note that," "It's worth mentioning that," "It goes without saying," "Needless to say" |
| Fake transitions | "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally," "Notably," "Subsequently," "Accordingly" |
| Hedging | "could potentially be considered," "in certain contexts," "to some extent," "generally speaking" |
| Corporate inflation | "leverage," "utilize," "synergy," "ecosystem," "paradigm shift," "value-added," "end-to-end solutions" |
| AI verbal tics | "delve," "embark," "tapestry," "testament," "underscore," "foster," "groundbreaking," "multifaceted," "bespoke," "hitherto," "realm" |
| Empty enthusiasm | "Certainly!," "Absolutely!," "Indeed!," "Revolutionary," "Game-changer," "Cutting-edge," "Innovative solutions" |
| AI narrative phrases | "Unlock the potential of," "Unleash the power of," "Delve into the world of," "Embark on a journey," "Pave the way for," "Harness the power of," "Bridging the gap between" |
| Human voice ban list | "Hope this email finds you well," "I wanted to reach out," "Just following up," "Please don't hesitate to reach out," "We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience," "We're thrilled/excited to announce" |

## Section 1: Quick Emails

### Scheduling / Confirmation

**BAD (AI-generated feel):**
> Hi Richard,
>
> I hope this email finds you well! I wanted to reach out to confirm our upcoming meeting. I believe 12PM CST would work wonderfully for both of us. Please don't hesitate to reach out if you need to make any adjustments to this arrangement.
>
> Best regards,
> Human

**GOOD:**
> Hi Richard,
>
> Thanks for making time today.
>
> 12PM CST works for me. Same meeting link?
>
> Regards,
> Human

**Why it works:** Two sentences of actual content. No filler. The question at the end is the only thing that requires a reply — everything else would just waste their time.

---

### Following Up on a Proposal

**BAD:**
> Hi Sarah,
>
> I hope you're doing well! I just wanted to follow up on the proposal I sent over last week. I understand you may be busy, and I certainly don't want to pressure you. I was just wondering if you had any thoughts or feedback you might like to share at your earliest convenience.
>
> Looking forward to hearing from you!
>
> Best regards,
> Human

**GOOD:**
> Hi Sarah,
>
> Any thoughts on the proposal?
>
> Happy to jump on a call if it's easier than email.
>
> Regards,
> Human

**Why it works:** "I just wanted to follow up" is the single most AI/corporate phrase in email. Cut it. The ask is the only sentence that matters. Put it first.

## Section 2: Product Announcements

### Feature Launch

**BAD:**
> We are thrilled and excited to announce that we have been working diligently to enhance our product offerings to better serve our valued customers and stakeholders. In today's rapidly evolving landscape, it is important to leverage cutting-edge solutions that drive meaningful outcomes. We are pleased to share that our new feature is now live.

**GOOD:**
> We shipped the new dashboard today.
>
> You can now see all your pages, their traffic, and conversion rates in one place. No more jumping between reports.
>
> Go check it out — feedback welcome.

**Why it works:** Leads with what happened. Explains what it means for the user. Ends with an invite, not a corporate bow.

---

### Product Update (Community Post)

**BAD:**
> Hey @everyone,
>
> We are excited to announce that we have been working on several exciting updates! We are committed to providing the best possible experience for our community. We look forward to your feedback as we continue on this journey together!

**GOOD:**
> Hey @everyone,
>
> Human here. It's been a busy few months — we shipped 14 updates since January and I haven't posted nearly enough about them.
>
> The big one: pages now load 40% faster on mobile. We rebuilt the render engine from scratch. It took longer than expected but it was worth it.
>
> There's a full changelog in the app. Please note it's still getting polished — a few edge cases we're still working through.
>
> Thanks for being patient. Let me know if anything feels off.

**Why it works:** Personal opening. Specific number. Honest about the delay. Ends with genuine invitation for feedback, not a corporate "we value your input."

## Section 3: Sales Pitches

### Discount Offer

**BAD:**
> Dear Valued Customer,
>
> We are pleased to present you with an exclusive offer tailored to meet your needs. After carefully considering your requirements, we would like to offer you a significant discount on our premium plan, which represents exceptional value for your investment.
>
> We look forward to the opportunity to serve you.

**GOOD:**
> For the Marketer plan what I can do is get it at $299/yr instead of the original $499/yr.
>
> That's close to a 40% discount — essentially $25/mo when billed annually.
>
> More than 50% off compared to the monthly plan.
>
> On top of that, I'll lock in the same price for as long as you stay with us.
>
> Let me know if you're interested and I'll get the upgrade sorted.

**Why it works:** One value point per line. Numbers do the talking. No enthusiasm inflation. The last line makes the next step obvious.

---

### Upsell During Support

**BAD:**
> Thank you for reaching out to us today! We truly value your business. As a valued customer, you may be interested in exploring our premium plan, which offers a comprehensive suite of innovative features designed to maximize your productivity and drive exceptional results.

**GOOD:**
> Quick question — are you on the Starter or Marketer plan?
>
> The reason I ask: what you're trying to do is actually easier on Marketer. It unlocks custom domains and the analytics you'd need for this.
>
> If you want I can check what upgrade would cost you.

**Why it works:** Asks before pitching. Frames the upgrade as a solution to their actual problem. Offers, doesn't push.

## Section 4: Handling Criticism and Complaints

### Response to a Support Complaint

**BAD:**
> Dear Customer,
>
> Thank you for reaching out to us. We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused. We take all customer feedback very seriously and have escalated this matter to our technical team for review. We assure you that we are committed to providing the highest quality of service.
>
> We thank you for your patience and understanding.

**GOOD:**
> Apologies for the trouble.
>
> What happened: the export function hit a timeout issue on files over 50MB. It's a known bug we've had in queue — your report pushed it up.
>
> Fix is live now. Try the export again and let me know if it works.

**Why it works:** Brief apology (not groveling). Explains what actually happened with technical credibility. States the fix. Ends with a test, not a corporate bow.

---

### Responding to a Downtime Complaint

**BAD:**
> We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience caused by the recent service disruption. We understand how important uninterrupted service is to our valued customers, and we take full responsibility for this unfortunate incident. We are committed to implementing robust measures to prevent future occurrences.

**GOOD:**
> Apologies for the downtime yesterday.
>
> A database migration ran longer than expected and took the app down for about 40 minutes. This is the first time we've had downtime of that length.
>
> We've identified the weak point in the migration process and are fixing it this week so it can't happen the same way again.
>
> Sorry again for the disruption.

**Why it works:** Specific time window. Honest about what happened. Acknowledges it's unusual. Commits to a specific fix. Second apology lands because the first one was backed by substance.

## Section 5: Correcting Misinformation

**BAD:**
> Thank you for raising this concern. We understand there may be some confusion regarding this topic, and we appreciate the opportunity to provide clarification. It's worth noting that there are multiple perspectives to consider in this situation.

**GOOD:**
> This is not true.
>
> You can connect your main domain — including a naked domain — by using www as your subdomain. Instructions are here: [link]
>
> Let me know if that doesn't work for your setup.

**Why it works:** States the correction directly. No hedging. Provides the fix immediately. Doesn't shame the person — just moves them forward.

## Section 6: Negotiation Emails

### Requesting Better Terms

**BAD:**
> I hope this message finds you well. I wanted to reach out regarding our current arrangement. I feel that, given our long-standing relationship, it might potentially be worth exploring whether there could be any flexibility in the terms we discussed.

**GOOD:**
> I want to revisit the contract terms before we sign.
>
> The 90-day payment clause puts us in a difficult position — our suppliers are on 30-day terms. Paying before we collect makes this hard to sustain.
>
> What I'd like: net-60 payment instead of net-90. That protects your interests too — we're more likely to stay healthy and keep the relationship long-term.
>
> Let me know if there's room to move on this. Happy to get on a call.

**Why it works:** Leads with the business problem, not an emotional ask. Uses specific numbers. Frames the ask as win-win. Clear next step.

---

### Responding to a Lowball Offer

**BAD:**
> Thank you so much for your offer! We truly appreciate your interest. Unfortunately, we are unable to accept the proposed terms at this time, as they do not align with our current pricing structure. We would love to explore possibilities that might work for both parties.

**GOOD:**
> I appreciate the offer but can't make that work at $800/mo.
>
> Our floor is $1,200. That's based on the actual support overhead for accounts at your usage level — below that we're running at a loss.
>
> If budget is the constraint, I can look at a quarterly billing structure that might help.

**Why it works:** Declines directly. Gives a number immediately. Explains why without being preachy. Offers one middle-ground option — doesn't just say no and stop.

## Section 7: Harsh Public Reviews

### 1-Star Review Response

**BAD:**
> Thank you so much for taking the time to share your feedback! We are truly sorry to hear about your experience. We take all customer feedback very seriously and are committed to continuous improvement. We would love the opportunity to make this right for you!

**GOOD:**
> Hi, thanks for sharing this — even if it's hard to read.
>
> Sad we couldn't win you over.
>
> The drag-and-drop issue you mentioned: we rebuilt that module in December. If you tried us before that, it was a legitimate problem and I understand the frustration.
>
> Could it be you were looking for something different? We're built for landing pages, not full websites — if you needed multi-page with a nav structure, we're probably not the right fit and I'd rather you know that upfront.
>
> For anyone reading this: if you're building landing pages or sales pages, come try us — 14-day free trial, no card required.

**Why it works:** Brief thanks without groveling. Acknowledges the emotion ("Sad we couldn't win you over"). Addresses the specific complaint with context. Reframes as a fit question without dismissing the review. Speaks to future readers directly.

## Section 8: Community Announcements

### Major Update Announcement

**BAD:**
> Hello Community Members,
>
> We are excited to announce several groundbreaking updates to our platform. Our dedicated team has been working tirelessly to bring you innovative features that will revolutionize your experience. We are committed to providing best-in-class solutions.

**GOOD:**
> Hi @everyone,
>
> Human here. I know it's been quiet from my side — been heads-down with the team on a big infrastructure upgrade.
>
> Starting today, all pages build 40% faster. For high-traffic pages, you'll notice it immediately.
>
> We also fixed the mobile editor bug that's been in the tracker since October (I know, I know — finally).
>
> Please note the speed improvements are still rolling out — you might not see them until tomorrow depending on your account.
>
> Thanks for bearing with us. More updates coming this month.

**Why it works:** Personal opener. Acknowledges the silence honestly. Specific metrics. Humor about the long-standing bug. Sets expectations for rollout. Ends with a forward-looking note.

---

### Pricing Change Announcement

**BAD:**
> Dear Valued Customers,
>
> We are writing to inform you of some exciting changes to our pricing structure. In order to continue delivering exceptional value and to sustain our commitment to innovation, we will be implementing new pricing tiers.

**GOOD:**
> Hi @everyone,
>
> Human here with something I know nobody loves to hear: we're updating our pricing in April.
>
> The short version: new accounts will pay 20% more. If you're already with us, nothing changes — your price is locked.
>
> Why: we haven't raised prices in three years and our infrastructure costs have doubled. We held off as long as we could.
>
> The full breakdown is at [link]. Read it — I wrote it myself, not marketing.
>
> Questions? Reply here or email me directly.

**Why it works:** Leads with the news, not context. Acknowledges it's unwelcome. Clear distinction between new and existing customers. Honest about the business reason. Invitation to reply creates accountability.

## Section 10: Explaining a Product Decision

### Removing a Feature

**BAD:**
> Our team has carefully evaluated the multifaceted considerations involved in this decision and has determined that, in order to provide a more streamlined experience, certain legacy features will be sunset in alignment with our strategic roadmap.

**GOOD:**
> We're removing the old export feature next month.
>
> Fewer than 2% of users were using it. It was creating bugs for everyone else and we couldn't maintain it without slowing down work on things people actually use.
>
> If you relied on it, here's what to do instead: [link]
>
> If that doesn't cover your use case, email me before March 15 and we'll work something out.

**Why it works:** States the decision up front. Gives the real reason with a real number. Provides the alternative. Creates a specific window for edge cases.

## Quick Reference: Side-by-Side Transformations

### Business update

**AI:** "We are excited to announce that we have been working diligently to enhance our product offerings to better serve our valued customers and stakeholders."

**Human:** "We shipped something today that took six months longer than expected. Here's what changed."

---

### Apology

**AI:** "We sincerely apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused. We take customer satisfaction very seriously."

**Human:** "Apologies for the trouble. Here's what happened and what we fixed."

---

### Saying no

**AI:** "Unfortunately, we are unable to accommodate this request at the current time due to various operational constraints."

**Human:** "Can't do that — it would break the export for everyone else. Here's what we can do instead."

---

### Asking for something

**AI:** "I was wondering if you might potentially be available to discuss this matter at your earliest convenience."

**Human:** "Free for a 15-minute call this week?"

## The Transformation Checklist

Before sending any communication:

1. **Read it out loud.** Stumble = rewrite.
2. **Would you say this to a friend?** If not, rewrite it as if you would.
3. **Cut every word that doesn't add information.** Adjectives restating the noun. Openers that delay the point.
4. **Is the main point in the first two sentences?** Move it there if not.
5. **Any banned phrases?** Check the list at the top.
6. **Passive voice?** Name who did what.
7. **Any vague category words?** Replace with the specific detail: number, date, name.
8. **Does it end with a clear next step?** Or does it trail off into corporate pleasantries?
